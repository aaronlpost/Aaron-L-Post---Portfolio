How did you get your start in web design?

Ever since I was a kid I have enjoyed building and creating. Legos, toy models, and drawing utensils were always a part of my childhood. But it was the blazing fast Packard Bell computer that my parents bought back in high school that brought me into the digital world. Occasionally, I experimented with Photoshop (5.0 LE!), and my web design interest followed when my father was interested in a site for his blooming clowning business. Why web design over architecture?

I love architecture and have huge respect for the profession, but ultimately I enjoy coding and design in the digital world more.

What are some of the parallels between designing a building and designing a website?

Ultimately in both professions, you're given a set of goals and constraints that you must reconcile creatively. I see architecture and web design as puzzles, where there is the freedom to pursue a solution through creativity, but also where certain criteria must be met in order to succeed. I love problem-solving, and I love creating. It's this intersection that has me so interested in all manners of design, physical and digital.
